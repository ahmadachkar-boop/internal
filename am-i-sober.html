📚 Understanding BAC

Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) is a measure of alcohol in your bloodstream. In Texas, it's illegal to drive with a BAC of 0.08% or higher.

BAC Levels & Effects

0.00-0.02% Normal behavior, minimal impairment
0.03-0.05% Mild relaxation, slight impairment
0.06-0.08% Impaired judgment and coordination
0.08%+ ILLEGAL to drive in Texas

Factors That Affect BAC

  • Body weight and composition
  • Biological sex (metabolism differences)
  • Food consumption
  • Drinking speed
  • Medications
  • Overall health

⏱️ How Long Until I'm Sober?

Your liver processes approximately one standard drink per hour. This rate cannot be accelerated by coffee, cold showers, or other remedies.

2 drinks ~2 hours to metabolize
4 drinks ~4 hours to metabolize
6 drinks ~6 hours to metabolize
8+ drinks ~8+ hours to metabolize

Important: Even if you feel sober, your BAC may still be above the legal limit. Always err on the side of caution.

⚠️ Important Legal Disclaimer

This calculator provides estimates only and should not be used to determine if you're safe to drive.

Many factors affect BAC including metabolism, medications, food intake, and health conditions. The only way to know your exact BAC is through official testing.

Never drink and drive. In Texas, you can be arrested for DWI with a BAC below 0.08% if you show signs of impairment. Penalties include fines up to $10,000, license suspension, and jail time.
