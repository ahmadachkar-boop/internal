üéí What to Bring (And What to Leave at Home)

‚úì DO BRING

  • Valid ID - You won't get in without it
  • Phone (fully charged) - With portable charger if possible
  • One debit/credit card - For drinks and emergencies
  • $20-40 cash - Some places are cash-only, tip bartenders
  • Chapstick/gum - Stay fresh
  • Hair tie - If you have long hair
  • Light jacket - Northgate can get cold at night
  • Comfortable shoes - You'll be walking and standing a lot

‚úó LEAVE AT HOME

  • Expensive jewelry - It can get lost or stolen
  • Multiple credit cards - Bring one, leave the rest
  • Car keys - If you're drinking, you're not driving
  • Passport - Driver's license is sufficient
  • Laptop/tablet - No reason to bring these
  • Large purse/bag - Easy to lose, hard to dance with
  • Expensive sunglasses - Night vision not required!
  • Anything irreplaceable - If you can't afford to lose it, don't bring it
Pro Tip: Use a crossbody bag or secure pockets with zippers. Don't leave drinks unattended, and keep your phone secured on you at all times.

üë• Group Safety Rules

Going out with friends? Follow these essential group safety guidelines:

The Buddy System

  • Never go anywhere alone
  • Bathroom trips = buddy required
  • Walking between bars = stay together
  • If someone leaves, everyone knows

Regular Check-Ins

  • Text group every 30-60 minutes
  • Share location if separated
  • Establish a meeting spot
  • Don't ghost your friends

Watch Your Friends

  • Monitor each other's drinking
  • Intervene if someone's too drunk
  • Don't let friends talk to strangers alone
  • Notice behavior changes

Arrive Together, Leave Together

  • Start the night as a group
  • End the night as a group
  • Everyone gets home safely
  • Confirm everyone's arrival home

Emergency Contact Plan

  • Share emergency contacts with group
  • Know where the nearest hospital is
  • Have CARPOOL saved: (979) 693-9905
  • Know when to call 911

Intervention Protocol

  • If friend is too drunk: get them home
  • If friend won't listen: take their keys
  • If friend is in danger: call 911
  • Better safe than sorry, always

‚ö†Ô∏è Never Leave Anyone Behind

The most dangerous situations happen when someone gets separated from their group. If you can't find a friend, don't leave until you do. Call them, check all locations, and if necessary, contact authorities.

üìç Northgate & College Station Specific Tips

Local knowledge for staying safe in the Bryan-College Station area:

Northgate District Safety

Peak Hours

  • Gets crowded after 10pm
  • Busiest: 11pm-2am
  • Bars close at 2am
  • Call CARPOOL by 1:30am to beat the rush

Parking

  • Park in well-lit areas
  • Use official lots when possible
  • Don't park in residential areas
  • CARPOOL will drive your car home

Walking Routes

  • Stick to well-lit main streets
  • Church Ave & Boyett St are safest
  • Avoid alleyways and shortcuts
  • Walk in groups, never alone

Local Resources

  • CARPOOL: (979) 693-9905
  • TAMU Police: (979) 845-2345
  • College Station PD: 911 or (979) 764-3600
  • Bryan PD: 911 or (979) 361-3888

Popular Northgate Venues

Main Entertainment Areas

Church Avenue Main strip with multiple bars, highest foot traffic
Boyett Street Parallel to Church, good alternative route
University Drive Main road - use crosswalks and stay alert
Parking Lots Well-lit municipal lots on Church and Boyett
College Station Transit: Texas A&M offers late-night bus routes on weekends. However, CARPOOL is specifically designed for safe rides home after drinking and will drive YOUR car home so you wake up with your vehicle.

Seasonal Considerations

Football Weekends

  • Northgate is PACKED on game days
  • Call CARPOOL early (longer waits)
  • Expect larger crowds and longer lines
  • Plan meeting spots in advance

Summer Nights

  • Stay hydrated - Texas heat is serious
  • Drink water between alcoholic drinks
  • Wear breathable clothing
  • Watch for heat exhaustion signs

Ring Dunks & Events

  • Special events = extra crowds
  • Reserve CARPOOL in advance if possible
  • Know alternate routes to avoid crowds
  • Plan for longer wait times

Weather Awareness

  • Check forecast before heading out
  • Rain = longer CARPOOL waits
  • Cold nights = bring a jacket
  • Storms = stay inside, delay trip

üåô During the Night: Stay Safe Rules

Follow these guidelines while you're out:

Drink Responsibly

  • Pace yourself: 1 drink per hour max
  • Alternate alcohol with water
  • Never leave your drink unattended
  • If you put a drink down, get a new one
  • Only accept drinks from bartenders
  • Watch drinks being made

Stay Connected

  • Keep phone charged and on you
  • Check in with friends regularly
  • Share your location
  • Answer when friends call/text
  • Don't let battery die

Be Aware

  • Know your surroundings at all times
  • Trust your instincts
  • If something feels wrong, leave
  • Stay in well-lit, populated areas
  • Don't engage with aggressive people

Protect Yourself

  • Keep ID and cards on you
  • Don't share personal info with strangers
  • Don't go home with someone you just met
  • Tell friends if you're leaving
  • Have exit strategy ready

Know Your Limits

  • Stop drinking if you feel dizzy
  • Sit down if you feel unwell
  • Tell friends if you need help
  • Don't let pride override safety
  • It's okay to call it a night early

Getting Home

  • Call CARPOOL before last call
  • Never drive or ride with drunk driver
  • Wait in safe, visible location
  • Verify driver/vehicle before getting in
  • Text someone when you get home
