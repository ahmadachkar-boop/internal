🚑 What to Do While Waiting for Help

Follow these critical steps after calling 911:

1

Call 911 First

Don't wait. Call immediately if you suspect alcohol poisoning. Tell them:

  • Your exact location
  • Person's age and symptoms
  • What they drank and how much (if known)
  • Any other substances involved
2

Position Them Safely

If unconscious or vomiting:

  • Turn them on their side (recovery position)
  • Keep head tilted to prevent choking
  • Support their back with a pillow or bundled clothing
3

Keep Them Warm

Alcohol poisoning can cause dangerous drops in body temperature.

  • Cover with blankets or jackets
  • Do not leave them outside in cold weather
  • Do not put them in a hot bath
4

Stay With Them

Never leave them alone. Monitor their condition constantly.

  • Check their breathing regularly
  • Watch for changes in skin color
  • Try to keep them awake and sitting up if conscious
  • Be prepared to perform CPR if needed
5

Prepare Information for Paramedics

Gather details to help emergency responders:

  • What and how much they drank
  • Time drinking started and ended
  • Any medications or drugs taken
  • Pre-existing medical conditions

✓ DO

  • Call 911 immediately
  • Turn them on their side
  • Keep them warm
  • Stay with them
  • Monitor breathing
  • Be honest with paramedics

✗ DON'T

  • Give them coffee or food
  • Put them in a cold shower
  • Make them vomit
  • Give them more alcohol
  • Let them "sleep it off"
  • Leave them alone

⚠️ Common Misconceptions That Can Be Deadly

  • "They just need to sleep it off" – BAC can continue rising even after drinking stops. They could die in their sleep.
  • "Coffee will sober them up" – Nothing speeds up alcohol metabolism. Only time works, and they may not have time.
  • "A cold shower will help" – This can cause shock and dangerous temperature drops.
  • "They'll be fine, they've been this drunk before" – Each episode is different. What was "fine" before could be fatal now.
  • "I don't want to get them in trouble" – Texas has Medical Amnesty laws protecting both the victim and caller from minor legal consequences.

⚖️ Texas Medical Amnesty Law

Don't let fear of legal consequences prevent you from calling for help.

Legal Protections When You Call 911

Under Texas law, both the person experiencing a medical emergency and the person seeking help are protected from minor alcohol-related charges if:

  • You call 911 or seek emergency medical assistance
  • You provide your name and location to authorities
  • You remain with the person until help arrives
  • The evidence of the offense was obtained as a result of seeking medical assistance
Texas A&M University Policy: Texas A&M supports the state's Medical Amnesty Law. Students who seek emergency medical help for themselves or others will not face university disciplinary action for alcohol-related violations. Your health and safety come first.

Bottom line: Saving a life is always more important than any potential legal or disciplinary consequences. When in doubt, call 911.
